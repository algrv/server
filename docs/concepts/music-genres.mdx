title: Music Genres
---

# Techno
**BPM**: 120-135 | **CPM**: 0.5-0.5625

Characterized by four-on-the-floor kick drum pattern, driving hi-hats, repetitive rhythms, minimal melodic elements, heavy use of synthesizers and drum machines. Focus on hypnotic, industrial textures.

# House
**BPM**: 120-130 | **CPM**: 0.5-0.5417

Steady four-to-the-floor kick drum, syncopated hi-hats and claps, warm basslines, soulful vocal samples, emphasis on groove and danceability. Often features piano chords and disco influences.

# Drum & Bass
**BPM**: 160-180 | **CPM**: 0.6667-0.75

Fast breakbeats (typically Amen break or similar), heavy sub-bass, complex drum patterns, rapid hi-hats. Characterized by the contrast between fast drums and slower, heavier bass.

# Dubstep
**BPM**: 140 | **CPM**: 0.5833

Half-time drums creating a slower feel despite tempo, wobble bass (LFO-modulated low-pass filter), sparse rhythmic structure, heavy sub-bass emphasis, syncopated rhythms. Dark, aggressive sound design.

# Hip Hop
**BPM**: 80-110 | **CPM**: 0.3333-0.4583

Boom-bap drum pattern (kick-snare emphasis), sampled loops and breaks, laid-back swing groove, emphasis on rhythm and vocal delivery. Often features chopped samples and scratching.

# Ambient
**BPM**: 60-90 | **CPM**: 0.25-0.375

Atmospheric pads and textures, minimal or absent percussion, slow-evolving soundscapes, emphasis on mood and space. Focus on creating immersive environments rather than rhythmic drive.

# Pop
**BPM**: 100-130 | **CPM**: 0.4167-0.5417

Catchy melodic hooks, clear verse-chorus-bridge structure, balanced mix emphasizing vocals, accessible harmonies, strong beat with simple drum patterns. Designed for mass appeal and radio play.

# Afro/Caribbean

## Afrobeat
**BPM**: 100-125 | **CPM**: 0.4167-0.5208

Complex polyrhythmic percussion layers, syncopated bass patterns, extended instrumental grooves, call-and-response vocals, horn section arrangements. West African rhythms fused with jazz and funk elements.

## Reggae
**BPM**: 60-90 | **CPM**: 0.25-0.375

One-drop rhythm (emphasis on beat 3), offbeat guitar or keyboard skanks, heavy bass presence, laid-back feel with syncopation. Characteristic "bubble" rhythm on keyboards, rimshots on snare.

## Dancehall
**BPM**: 90-110 | **CPM**: 0.375-0.4583

Digital riddims (rhythm tracks), syncopated drum patterns, heavy sub-bass, energetic percussion, sparse arrangement. Faster, more aggressive evolution of reggae with electronic production.

## Amapiano
**BPM**: 110-115 | **CPM**: 0.4583-0.4792

Log drum kick pattern (distinctive syncopated kick rhythm), jazzy piano chord progressions, percussive bassline, South African house influence. Combination of deep house, jazz, and kwaito elements with shuffled hi-hats.
